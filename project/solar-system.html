🪐 Solar System Gallery

Explore our Sun and planets — from fiery giants to icy worlds, arranged in their cosmic order.

☀️ Sun

The heart of our solar system — a glowing ball of hydrogen and helium that provides light and warmth for life on Earth.

☿ Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun. Days are scorching hot, but nights are freezing cold.

♀ Venus

Covered in thick clouds of acid, Venus is hotter than Mercury due to its extreme greenhouse effect.

🌕 Moon

Earth's only natural satellite, the Moon influences our tides and has a surface covered in craters.

🌍 Earth

Our blue home — the only known planet with life, protected by a rich atmosphere and magnetic field.

♂ Mars

The red planet, with vast canyons and the tallest volcano in the solar system — Olympus Mons.

♃ Jupiter

The gas giant king, massive enough to fit all other planets inside. Its Great Red Spot is a storm older than Earth’s recorded history.

♄ Saturn

Famous for its dazzling rings, Saturn is a gas giant that could float in water due to its low density.

♅ Uranus

Tilts on its side, spinning like a rolling ball. Its pale blue hue comes from icy methane clouds.

♆ Neptune

The farthest planet, wrapped in deep blue storms with winds faster than any other planet in the solar system.
